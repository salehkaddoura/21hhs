Medical Social Workers Offer Support

When our members are having a difficult time adjusting that is beyond a physical level, social workers can help find solutions to get back on track. They’re here to make sure and understand that such reactions are common, that nobody is alone and he or she can get you the help you need. One of the most important services a home social worker provides is educating and giving emotional support to caregivers who often find their new roles stressful and overwhelming. 21st Century Home Health Services’ social workers are trained to listen to caregivers. Our medical social workers offer the support and resources patients need in order to recover from illness or trauma as well as it’s psychological, emotional and physical consequences. Sometimes this means helping a client with practical tasks such as cooking and transportation after a hospital stay. At other times, it involves giving psychosocial support to crime victims, helping law enforcement in child abuse cases or arranging grief counseling. Our staff encourages caregivers to take care of themselves and to avoid isolation by talking to people in similar situations in a support group or by seeking support from family and friends. They can tell them about training and classes that are available to help them learn new skills and to find out more about what they’re dealing with.
